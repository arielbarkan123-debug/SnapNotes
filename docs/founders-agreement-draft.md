## 2. RECITALS (BACKGROUND)

**WHEREAS** the Developer has independently conceived, designed, and built the technology platform known as "X+1" (accessible at xplus1.ai and related domains), investing approximately 800–1,000 hours of personal development work prior to this Agreement;

**NOW THEREFORE**, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 3. THE VENTURE

3.1. **The Product.** "X+1" is an AI-powered educational technology platform that transforms study materials (images, PDFs, documents) into interactive, structured courses with spaced repetition, practice exams, and personalized learning. The platform is accessible at xplus1.ai and any related domains, subdomains, or successor URLs.

3.2. **Legal Entity.** The Parties intend to incorporate an Israeli company (Ltd. / בע"מ) under the laws of the State of Israel within **90 days** of signing this Agreement (the "Company"). Until the Company is formed, this Agreement governs the relationship between the Parties directly.

3.3. **Transfer to Company.** Upon incorporation, the Parties shall transfer all relevant assets (intellectual property, domain names, accounts, agreements) to the Company in exchange for shares reflecting the ownership percentages set out in Section 5 below. This Agreement shall then serve as the basis for the Company's Articles of Association (תקנון).

---

## 4. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1. The Developer's Responsibilities:
- (a) All technology development, architecture, coding, and maintenance of the platform
- (b) Product vision, roadmap, and feature decisions
- (c) Technical infrastructure management (hosting, databases, APIs, deployments)
- (d) Technical hiring decisions (subject to budget approval per Section 7)
- (e) Data security and privacy compliance (technical implementation)

### 4.2. The Investors' Responsibilities:
- (a) All financial investment required for the operation and growth of the venture
- (b) Marketing strategy, execution, and customer acquisition
- (c) Business development, partnerships, and sales
- (d) Financial management, bookkeeping, and accounting
- (e) Legal and regulatory compliance (business-side)
- (f) Funding any external contractors, services, or employees as needed

### 4.3. Exclusivity and Commitment.
- (a) The Developer commits to working on X+1 as his primary technology project. The Developer may pursue personal projects, schoolwork, and other non-competing activities.
- (b) The Investors commit to actively working on the marketing and business growth of X+1 and shall not invest in or promote a directly competing product during the term of this Agreement.

---

## 5. OWNERSHIP AND EQUITY

5.1. **Ownership Split.** The venture shall be owned as follows:
- The Developer: **50%** (fifty percent)
- The Investors (jointly): **50%** (fifty percent)

5.2. **Nature of Ownership.** This ownership applies to:
- (a) All shares in the future Company
- (b) All profits and revenue generated by the venture
- (c) All proceeds from any sale, merger, acquisition, or liquidation of the venture

5.3. **Equal Stake.** The Investors' 50% stake is held jointly between Investor 1 and Investor 2 as a single block. The internal division between them is their private matter and does not affect the 50/50 split with the Developer.

5.4. **No Dilution Without Consent.** Neither Party's ownership may be diluted without their written consent. Any future issuance of equity (to employees, advisors, or new investors) must be approved by both Parties and shall dilute both Parties equally unless otherwise agreed in writing.

---

## 6. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

6.1. **Pre-Existing IP.** The Parties acknowledge that:
- (a) The Developer is the sole creator and owner of all code, software, algorithms, designs, user interfaces, database structures, and technical documentation created prior to this Agreement (collectively, "Pre-Existing Technology").
- (b) The Investors are the registered owners of the domain name "xplus1.ai" and any related domains they have purchased (collectively, "Domain Assets").

6.2. **License to the Venture.** Upon signing this Agreement:
- (a) The Developer grants the venture an exclusive, irrevocable, worldwide license to use the Pre-Existing Technology for the purposes of operating and commercializing X+1.
- (b) The Investors grant the venture an exclusive, irrevocable license to use the Domain Assets for the purposes of operating X+1.

6.3. **Transfer to Company.** Upon incorporation of the Company (Section 3.2):
- (a) The Developer shall assign all Pre-Existing Technology and future technology IP to the Company.
- (b) The Investors shall transfer the Domain Assets to the Company.
- (c) From that point forward, all IP shall be owned by the Company.

6.4. **Future IP.** All intellectual property created by either Party in connection with X+1 after the date of this Agreement shall belong to the venture (and, upon incorporation, to the Company).

6.5. **Developer's Moral Rights.** The Developer retains recognition as the original creator/architect of the X+1 platform for purposes of personal portfolio, resume, and professional reputation.

---

## 7. FINANCIAL CONTRIBUTIONS AND EXPENSES

7.1. **Prior Investment.** The Parties acknowledge that:
- (a) The Developer has contributed approximately 800–1,000 hours of development work, valued as sweat equity, which is reflected in the 50% ownership stake.
- (b) The Investors have already contributed **5,000 NIS** (five thousand New Israeli Shekels) toward the venture's expenses.

7.2. **Upcoming Investment.** The Investors commit to investing an additional **30,000–45,000 NIS** (thirty thousand to forty-five thousand New Israeli Shekels) for contracted services in the near term, the exact amount to be determined based on the engagement.

7.3. **Future Expenses.** All ongoing and future expenses (marketing, hosting, services, contractors, legal, accounting, etc.) shall be funded by the Investors unless otherwise agreed in writing. The Developer is not required to make financial contributions.

7.4. **Expense Approval.**
- (a) Expenses up to **5,000 NIS** per month may be approved by either Party independently.
- (b) Expenses exceeding **5,000 NIS** per single expenditure require approval from both Parties.
- (c) All expenses shall be documented and made available to both Parties.

7.5. **Reimbursement.** The Investors' financial contributions are equity investments, not loans. They shall not be repaid separately but are reflected in the 50% ownership stake. If the venture generates profit, it shall be distributed per Section 8.

7.6. **Developer Compensation.** The Developer shall work on an equity-only basis initially. If the venture reaches a revenue milestone that both Parties agree justifies it, a monthly salary or stipend for the Developer may be established by mutual written agreement.

---

## 8. PROFIT DISTRIBUTION

8.1. Until the Company is formed, any net profit (revenue minus expenses) shall be split **50/50** between the Developer and the Investors.

8.2. After incorporation, profit distribution shall follow the Company's Articles of Association, reflecting the same 50/50 split.

8.3. The Parties may mutually agree to reinvest profits into the business instead of distributing them.

---

## 9. DECISION MAKING

9.1. **Mutual Decisions.** Major decisions require the agreement of both Parties. Major decisions include:
- (a) Raising external funding or taking on debt
- (b) Selling, merging, or dissolving the venture
- (c) Hiring full-time employees
- (d) Entering contracts exceeding 10,000 NIS in value
- (e) Changing the core product direction or target market
- (f) Any expenditure exceeding 5,000 NIS per single item

9.2. **Domain-Specific Authority.** Within their respective domains:
- (a) The Developer has final say on technical decisions (architecture, technology stack, development priorities, technical hires).
- (b) The Investors have final say on marketing and business decisions (marketing channels, pricing strategy, business partnerships).

9.3. **Deadlock Resolution.** If the Parties cannot agree on a major decision after good-faith discussion, they shall seek mediation through a mutually agreed mediator in Israel before taking any unilateral action.

---

## 10. EXIT AND TRANSFER

### 10.1. Developer Exit
- (a) The Developer may cease active development at any time by providing **60 days' written notice**.
- (b) Upon exit, the Developer **retains** his full 50% ownership stake.
- (c) The Developer shall provide reasonable transition support (documentation, knowledge transfer) during the notice period.
- (d) The Developer shall not delete, sabotage, or withhold access to any code, accounts, or systems belonging to the venture.

### 10.2. Investor Exit
- (a) The Investors may withdraw from the venture at any time by providing **60 days' written notice**.
- (b) Upon exit, the Investors **retain** their full 50% ownership stake.
- (c) The Investors shall transfer all domain names, marketing accounts, and business assets to the venture/Developer during the notice period.

### 10.3. Buyout Option
- (a) If either Party wishes to exit AND sell their stake, the other Party has a **Right of First Refusal** — the right to purchase the departing Party's stake at a price determined by an independent valuator agreed upon by both Parties, or by a formula to be agreed upon at the time.
- (b) If the remaining Party does not exercise the Right of First Refusal within 60 days, the departing Party may sell to a third party at the same or higher price.

### 10.4. Sale of the Venture
- (a) The venture may only be sold in its entirety with the written consent of both Parties.
- (b) Sale proceeds shall be split 50/50.

---

## 11. NON-COMPETE AND CONFIDENTIALITY

11.1. **Non-Compete.** During the term of this Agreement and for **12 months** after either Party's exit:
- (a) Neither Party shall create, fund, or actively participate in a directly competing product (an AI-powered educational platform converting study materials into interactive courses) in the Israeli or US markets.
- (b) This does not restrict either Party from working in education technology generally, only from creating a substantially similar product.

11.2. **Confidentiality.** Each Party shall keep confidential all non-public information about the venture, including but not limited to: source code, business plans, financial data, user data, marketing strategies, and investor discussions. This obligation survives the termination of this Agreement.

11.3. **Exceptions.** Confidentiality does not apply to information that: (a) is publicly available, (b) was known to the Party before this Agreement, (c) is required to be disclosed by law, or (d) is disclosed with the other Party's written consent.

13.2. **Surviving Provisions.** Sections 6 (IP), 11 (Non-Compete and Confidentiality), and 12 (Representations) shall survive the termination of this Agreement.
