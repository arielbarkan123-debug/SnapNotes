# Competitive Landscape Brief — X+1 vs EdTech Study Apps (April 2026)

> Research brief by agent #1 of 4. Sources at bottom.

## 1. The Big Ten Competitors — At a Glance

### Photomath (Google)
**Price:** Core scanning/solving is free. Photomath Plus is **$9.99/month or $69.99/year** ($5.83/mo equiv.). **Positioning:** The OG math scanner — point camera at equation, get step-by-step solution. **What students love:** Instant camera-to-answer, clean UI, the step-by-step breakdown genuinely teaches. **Failure mode:** Math-only. No science, no humanities. No flashcards, no exam simulation, no personalization. Post-Google acquisition it has stagnated — product direction is unclear and features have barely moved since 2024. **Trajectory:** Flat-to-declining as Gauth eats the camera-scan market. **Opening for X+1:** Photomath is a single-feature product; X+1 is a full study stack.

### Gauth / GauthMath (ByteDance)
**Price:** Free tier, Premium ~$9.99/mo (unlimited AI solutions, tutor guidance, 100M+ solved Q library), Tutor plan $19.99/mo (adds live human tutors), ~$99.99/year. **Positioning:** TikTok-native homework solver — 30+ subjects in 50+ languages, AI + human tutor combo. **What students love:** 95% solve rate, 4.9/5 App Store rating on 1.67M+ reviews, viral TikTok content. **Failure mode:** Answer-first, not learning-first. Generic course structure. ByteDance regulatory risk in US — app was briefly suspended in Jan 2024. **Trajectory:** Exploding — 200M users by 2024, held #1 US education app download for six consecutive days in Q3 2025, behind only Duolingo. **Wake-up call:** Gauth is the 800-pound gorilla — they have distribution X+1 can't match.

### Socratic by Google
**Price:** 100% free, no premium tier. **Positioning:** Google's free homework helper — Algebra, Geometry, Trig, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, History, Literature. **What students love:** Totally free, no paywalls, solid subject coverage. **Failure mode:** Barely maintained — product almost untouched since 2023. No AI tutor conversations, no flashcards, no exam prep. Google is clearly prioritizing Gemini over Socratic. **Trajectory:** Flat and slowly becoming legacy. **Opening for X+1:** Socratic is the "free floor" students anchor against — X+1 must show meaningfully more value than "free + Google brand."

### Brainly
**Price:** Brainly Plus ~$18/6 months or $24/year (<$1/day), 7-day free trial (auto-converts). Brainly Tutor is the premium tier adding step-by-step explanations in math, physics, biology, chemistry. **Positioning:** Community Q&A + AI ("Ginny") — hybrid of user-generated answers, "experts," and AI. **What students love:** Crowd-sourced breadth, cheap entry price. **Failure mode:** Not classic 1:1 tutoring — it's Q&A scraping with AI polish. Quality varies wildly between community answers. Trial is a dark pattern (auto-charge). **Trajectory:** Growing on volume but margin pressure as AI commoditizes answers. **Opening for X+1:** Brainly wins on price anchor ($24/year!) — X+1 must justify a higher price through actual learning, not just answers.

### Chegg
**Price:** Chegg Study $14.95/mo or $74.95/yr; Chegg Study Pack $19.95/mo or $119.95/yr (adds Math Solver, Writing Tool, multi-LLM comparison, video guides). **Positioning:** Legacy textbook solutions + expert Q&A — the incumbent. **What students love:** Historical Q&A library depth (millions of solved textbook problems). **Failure mode:** GETTING ANNIHILATED. Stock dropped 99% from $113 to ~$0.81. Lost 500K+ subscribers, down 31% to 3.2M. Revenue down 30–40% YoY. Laid off 45% of workforce (636 people) in 2025. Suing Google over AI. 89% of students now use ChatGPT instead. **Trajectory:** Existential decline. **Wake-up call:** Chegg is X+1's ghost-of-Christmas-future if pricing is high and differentiation is weak — ChatGPT killed their $15/mo answer library.

### Course Hero (Learneo)
**Price:** $9.95–$39.99/mo across Premier and Premier Plus tiers. Plus tier bundles AI tools + partner services (includes Symbolab). **Positioning:** College-focused document library + AI Chat-with-PDF — study materials marketplace. **What students love:** "Unblur" hacks, exam-specific docs, tutor marketplace. **Failure mode:** College students, not K-12. Also commoditized by ChatGPT — charging $40/mo for what Claude/GPT does free. **Trajectory:** Flat. Learneo IPO prep reportedly shelved. **Opening for X+1:** Not a direct threat in the 12–18 demographic Ariel targets.

### Quizlet
**Price:** Quizlet Plus ~$7.99/mo or $35.99/year (with variation: some report $9.99/mo or $44.99/year). 7-day trial; teachers get 30 days. **Positioning:** Flashcards + AI — the studying verb. Q-Chat (Socratic-method AI tutor) + Magic Notes (text/notes → flashcards) + Brain Beats (flashcards → songs). **What students love:** Brand ubiquity in US schools, fast flashcard sharing, gamified review. **Failure mode:** Still fundamentally a flashcards product. AI features feel bolted on. Low engagement outside of test weeks. **Trajectory:** Flat — peaked in 2021–2022 pandemic era. **Opening for X+1:** Quizlet treats flashcards as the whole product; X+1 uses flashcards as ONE pillar of a learning stack. $36/year is a clear price anchor.

### Duolingo Max
**Price:** $29.99/mo or $168/year (Family $240/year, up to 6). **Positioning:** AI conversational practice — Roleplay + Video Call with Lily (the AI character) + Explain My Answer (now free). **What students love:** Genuinely useful Video Call feature for speaking practice. **Failure mode:** Reviewers openly note that Max's AI conversation quality "is comparable to free AI tools" — the Super→Max upgrade is effectively $84/yr for Video Call + Roleplay. Duolingo hasn't published Max conversion rates publicly. **Trajectory:** Growing but not breaking out. **Relevance to X+1:** This is the premium-AI price ceiling in consumer EdTech. Nobody else charges $168/yr for AI features to consumers. X+1 should NOT price here.

### Khan Academy + Khanmigo
**Price:** Khan Academy core is free forever. Khanmigo is free for teachers; **$4/mo or $44/year for parents/learners.** Parent plan includes up to 10 child accounts. **Positioning:** Nonprofit AI tutor built on GPT-4 — trusted, safe, education-first. **What students love:** Brand trust, pedagogically sound, affordability. Parents love the price. **Failure mode:** Limited subject depth for exam prep, no Hebrew localization, no gamification layer that teen students actually engage with. **Trajectory:** Growing slowly — nonprofit distribution via schools. **Wake-up call:** $44/year is the "trustworthy affordable" anchor. Any price Ariel sets above $44/year must answer "why not just use Khanmigo?"

### Newer AI-Native Entrants
- **Mathos AI:** $4.99/week. 5M+ students. Aggressive weekly pricing model. Math-only.
- **Solvely:** $9.99/mo (annual) or $19.99/mo, 10M+ students. Multi-subject homework helper.
- **StudyFetch:** $8–12/mo. 6M+ students. $11.5M Series A (mid-2025) led by Owl Ventures + College Board — investor validation. All-in-one study platform — this is the closest US analog to X+1.
- **Cluely:** $20/mo or $100/year ("cheat on everything" brand, now rebranded as AI meeting assistant). $20.3M raised (a16z-backed). 70K signups week one. CEO publicly retracted a fabricated $7M ARR claim. Suffered data breach (83K users). Not a direct competitor but a cautionary tale on EdTech hype.

## 2. The 3 Pricing Anchors Most Relevant to X+1

**Anchor A — "Trustworthy Affordable" ($44/year, Khanmigo):** This is the price that parents say yes to without thinking. Anything above it needs a story. Hebrew localization + Israeli curriculum + richer interactive diagrams is a defensible story.

**Anchor B — "Standard Premium Study App" ($70–$100/year, Photomath / Gauth / Brainly / Solvely):** This is the pricing graveyard — most consumer study apps cluster here at ~$5–10/mo. This is where X+1 will land by default if priced naively. Expect churn.

**Anchor C — "All-in-One Study Stack" ($120–$168/year, Chegg Study Pack / Duolingo Max):** Only works when you credibly cover an entire workflow (homework, study, exams, flashcards) AND can justify it against free AI. Chegg proves the risk — they were $120/yr and lost 31% of subs. Duolingo pulls it off because Super + Max has no real consumer equivalent.

**Recommendation starting point:** Target **$59–79/year** ($5.99–7.99/mo month-to-month, $59 annual). This sits above Khanmigo (justifying it with breadth + Hebrew) but meaningfully below Chegg/Duolingo Max (not fighting an unwinnable premium war). Run A/B on $49 vs $79 annual to find price elasticity.

## 3. The Missing Segment — What X+1 Could Own

**Hebrew-first, bagrut-aware, 12–18 study companion with interactive diagrams.** Not a single competitor on this list ships Hebrew UI + RTL + Israeli curriculum awareness as a first-class experience. Gauth has 50 languages but Israel isn't a focus market; Khan has some Hebrew content but no Hebrew AI tutor; Brainly has some Hebrew Q&A but no native app flow. The Israeli EdTech market is $1.2B with 163 active K-12 companies — and yet the global AI-native study category is essentially absent in Hebrew.

Underneath that: **parent-paid with transparent, boring pricing** (no 7-day trial dark patterns, no ByteDance data questions, no Chegg-style answer dumps). Israeli parents are culturally willing to pay for education — reframe X+1 as "the AI tutor for bagrut prep and daily school, in Hebrew" and you have a moat competitors can't cheaply copy.

## 4. The Red Zones — Where NOT to Play

**a) English-market math scanning.** Photomath + Gauth + Mathos have this locked. Gauth especially is spending TikTok money X+1 cannot match. Don't compete on "take a photo of math, get answer."

**b) Flashcards-as-hero-product.** Quizlet has the brand, the network effect (shared sets), and a decade of SEO. Flashcards should be a feature of X+1, never the headline.

**c) College-market document marketplaces.** Course Hero + Chegg + Studocu own this — and ChatGPT is killing them anyway. Don't chase college; stay 12–18.

**d) Sub-$5/week "cheap-and-cheerful" weekly subscriptions.** Mathos AI's $4.99/week works because it's an impulse buy for panic homework. This model cannibalizes LTV and gives you nothing for Israeli parent buyers.

## 5. The Wake-Up Calls — Things That Should Make Ariel Nervous

**1. Gauth's distribution.** 200M users, #1 US education download, TikTok-native. If Gauth localizes aggressively for Hebrew (they're already 50+ languages) in 2026, X+1's window narrows. **Mitigation:** Ship Hebrew excellence + Israeli curriculum depth now; win schools before Gauth arrives.

**2. StudyFetch's investor signal.** $11.5M Series A led by Owl Ventures + College Board in mid-2025. That's the same investor class Ariel would pitch. They've chosen StudyFetch's all-in-one thesis as the US horse. X+1 needs a differentiated investor story — "winning non-English, non-US markets" is that story.

**3. The Chegg cautionary tale.** A $120/year subscription business lost 99% market cap in three years because ChatGPT is free. Any pricing strategy that doesn't explicitly answer "why not just use ChatGPT for free?" is already dead. X+1's answer must be: curriculum-aligned + Hebrew + interactive diagrams + structured learning, not raw Q&A.

**4. Khanmigo at $44/year.** A nonprofit with GPT-4, teacher trust, and global distribution is the hardest competitor to out-value. The ONLY way X+1 beats Khan on value is vertical depth — Hebrew, bagrut, visual interactivity, homework-check tuned to Israeli textbooks.

**5. The free-AI floor keeps rising.** Claude/GPT/Gemini are now free-tier good enough to solve most homework. "Answer quality" is no longer defensible. Defensibility = structured learning product (courses, SRS, exams, diagrams), localization (Hebrew, RTL, bagrut), and distribution (schools, parents, teachers).

## Bottom Line for a 24-Hour Pricing Decision

Price annual at **$59 (₪219)** and monthly at **$7.99 (₪29.90)**. Offer a 7-day free trial WITHOUT card capture (differentiate from Brainly's dark-pattern trial). Add a Family plan at **$99/year** for up to 3 kids (undercut Khanmigo's family economics, reward multi-child households — common in Israel). Skip the high-premium tier for now; adding tutoring later is optional ammo. Anchor Hebrew-market marketing against Khanmigo ("local + deeper"), English-market marketing against Gauth ("learning, not answers"). Avoid Chegg's mistake: never market X+1 as an answer engine — always as a learning engine.
