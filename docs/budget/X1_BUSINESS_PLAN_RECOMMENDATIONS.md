### The 800lb gorilla: Gauth

- 200M users, #1 US Education app downloads Q3 2025 (6 consecutive days)
- TikTok-native growth via [Home From College creator program](https://www.homefromcollege.com/blog/case-studies/gauth) — paying student creators flat fee + performance bonus

- Pricing: Free / $9.99/mo Premium / $19.99/mo Tutor
- **Weakness:** Answer-first not learning-first. Not Hebrew-excellent. Regulatory/ByteDance risk. Generic course structure.

**What this means:** Do NOT compete on "camera scans math, gets answer" in English. That market is lost. **Compete on Hebrew excellence + curriculum alignment + structured learning (courses, SRS, exams, diagrams) — what Gauth refuses to do because it slows their growth.**

### The cautionary tale: Chegg

$113 → $0.81 in 3 years. Lost 500K subs. Laid off 45% of workforce. Suing Google over AI. Reason: **they charged $120/year for textbook solutions that ChatGPT now does free.**

**Lesson:** Any pricing strategy that doesn't answer "why not just use ChatGPT for free?" is dead on arrival. X+1's answers:
1. **Curriculum-aligned** (Bagrut, AP, GCSE — not generic math)
2. **Homework-check not homework-do** (teachers accept it)
3. **Structured learning stack** (courses + SRS + practice + exams + diagrams, not just Q&A)
4. **Hebrew + RTL + Israeli parent trust**
